#cfgmgmtcamp

1 and 2 February 2016

Gent, Belgium

Next Generation Config Mgmt.

James Shubin (purpleidea)

UPDATED DATE/TIME/LOCATION

Main Track (Future Tooling) - Monday: 01/Feb/2016 16:20 - D.AUD

Video Recording

"A prototype for a next generation config management tool, and the specific problems this design solves. Three of the main design features of the tool include:

  1. Parallel execution
  2. Event driven mechanism
  3. Distributed architecture
This talk will demo a prototype I've built that implements these ideas. It is written in golang, and is completely free software. This talk will feature working code and a number of live demos!"

An introductory blog post on the subject is available. Attendees are encouraged to read it before the talk if they are interested!

About James Shubin (purpleidea)

James Shubin is best known for his work on Configuration Management, his Technical Blog, Oh-My-Vagrant, (a tool he started) and other related DevOps friendly projects. Most of his writing happens on: The Technical Blog of James (https://ttboj.wordpress.com/), but he sometimes says things as @purpleidea on Twitter, and he currently works for Red Hat doing research and prototyping within systems engineering. He studied Physiology at university and sometimes likes to talk about cardiology.

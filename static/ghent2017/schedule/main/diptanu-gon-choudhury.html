#cfgmgmtcamp

6 and 7 February 2017

Gent, Belgium

Taming the Wild West: A journey from Puppet to Distributed Schedulers and Containers

Diptanu Gon Choudhury , Monday February 6, 16:20 -17:00 , D.AUD

The last ten years have been monumental in the Web Operations space. As the need for higher availability and reliability of internet scale services has continued to grow, we have seen a massive evolution in the practices and tools concerning web operations. In this talk, we will explore the needs that dictate the use of Distributed Resource Schedulers over configuration management tools like Puppet and Chef for the last mile orchestration and cluster supervision on public and private clouds. Needless to say that the days of configuration management tools are not over, so we will explore how tools like Puppet and Chef fits in with cluster schedulers and linux containers.

About Speaker

Diptanu is a Senior Engineer at HashiCorp, and works on large-scale distributed systems, cluster schedulers, service discovery and highly available and high throughput systems on the public cloud. He is a core committer to the Nomad cluster scheduler which has a parallel and distributed scheduler and support heterogeneous virtualized workloads. Prior to HashiCorp, Diptanu worked in the Cloud Platform group at Netflix, where he worked on the core platform infrastructure that powered the Microservices infrastructure of Netflix. He worked on Apache Mesos and wrote a cluster scheduler for running clusters of Docker containers on AWS, and also contributed to various reactive IPC and service discovery infrastructure projects.
